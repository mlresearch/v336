title: A Distribution Testing Approach to Clustering Distributions
section: Original Papers
abstract: 'We study the following distribution clustering problem: Given a hidden
  partition of $k$ distributions into $2$ groups, such that the distributions within
  each group are the same, and the distributions associated with the clusters are
  pairwise $\varepsilon$-far in total variation, the goal is to recover the partition.
  We establish upper and lower bounds on the sample complexity for two fundamental
  cases: (1) when one of the cluster’s distributions is known, and (2) when both are
  unknown. Our upper and lower bounds characterize the sample complexity’s dependence
  on the domain size $n$, number of distributions $k$, size $r$ of one of the clusters,
  and distance $\varepsilon$. In particular, we achieve tightness with respect to
  $(n,k,r,\varepsilon)$ (up to an $O(\log k)$ factor) for all regimes.  In addition,
  we show that this result extends to the case of $d$-clustering for any constant
  number of clusters $d$.'
