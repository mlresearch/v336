abstract: 'We study the problem of selecting the largest among $n$ unknown values
  $x_1,…,x_n$ given only a single unbiased estimate $y_i$ for each $x_i$. We design
  strategies that are simultaneously admissible (not uniformly dominated by any other
  strategy) and also never worse than a given baseline such as uniform random selection.
  We provide an application to stochastic optimization, where we obtain online-to-batch
  conversion bounds with a desirable “no-compromise” guarantee: they are never worse
  than standard random iterate selection, and yet can be significantly better in benign
  settings.'
