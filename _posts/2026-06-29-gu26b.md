title: Functional Stochastic Localization
section: Original Papers
abstract: Eldan’s stochastic localization is a probabilistic construction that has
  proved instrumental to modern breakthroughs in high-dimensional geometry and the
  design of sampling algorithms. Motivated by sampling under non-Euclidean geometries
  and the mirror descent algorithm in optimization, we develop a functional generalization
  of Eldan’s process that replaces Gaussian regularization with regularization by
  any positive integer multiple of a log-Laplace transform. We further give a mixing
  time bound on the Markov chain induced by our localization process, which holds
  if our target distribution satisfies a functional Poincaré inequality. Finally,
  we apply our framework to differentially private convex optimization in $\ell_p$
  norms for $p \in [1, 2)$, where we improve state-of-the-art query complexities in
  a zeroth-order model.
